## Electronic Component Reliability Analysis using MIL-HDBK-217F

## Overview

This project presents a reliability analysis of electronic components using the MIL-HDBK-217F reliability prediction methodology. The objective was to evaluate how environmental conditions, operating temperature, and electrical stress influence component reliability and expected operational lifetime.

The study focused on:

- Electrolytic Tantalum Capacitor (CSR)
- Ceramic Capacitor (CKR)
- MOSFET Transistor

For each component, failure rate (λ), Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF), and Failures In Time (FIT) were calculated and compared under multiple environmental and operating conditions.

---

## Objectives

- Apply the MIL-HDBK-217F reliability prediction standard.
- Evaluate reliability performance under different environments.
- Analyze temperature effects on component lifetime.
- Study the influence of electrical stress on reliability.
- Compare reliability metrics across different electronic components.
- Interpret engineering results and identify dominant degradation factors.

---

## Methodology

The analysis was conducted using the MIL-HDBK-217F reliability prediction model.

The following reliability metrics were evaluated:

### Failure Rate (λ)

Represents the expected number of failures per million operating hours.

### Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)

Represents the average operational lifetime before failure.

### FIT (Failures In Time)

Represents failures per billion operating hours.

### Environmental Conditions

The following environments were considered:

| Environment | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| GB | Ground Benign |
| GM | Ground Mobile |
| NS | Naval Sheltered |

### Operating Conditions

The study evaluated:

- Multiple temperatures
- Different operating voltages
- Environmental stress factors
- Component-specific reliability parameters

---

## Components Studied

### 1. Tantalum Electrolytic Capacitor (CSR)

Analysis included:

- Temperature effect (25°C, 45°C, 85°C)
- Environmental influence
- Voltage stress influence
- MTBF comparison
- FIT analysis

### Key Findings

- Ground Benign environment produced the highest reliability.
- Increasing temperature increased failure rate.
- MTBF decreased significantly at elevated temperatures.
- Higher operating voltage increased electrical stress and reduced lifetime.

---

### 2. Ceramic Capacitor (CKR)

Analysis included:

- Temperature variation
- Environmental impact
- Reliability comparison across operating conditions

### Key Findings

- Ceramic capacitors exhibited high reliability under benign conditions.
- Mechanical vibration and thermal cycling significantly increased failure rates.
- Humidity and salt exposure negatively affected reliability.

---

### 3. MOSFET Reliability Analysis

Reliability calculations were performed using:

- Junction temperature analysis
- Environmental factors
- Activation energy models
- Temperature acceleration factors

### Key Findings

- Failure rate increased exponentially with junction temperature.
- Ground Benign environment provided the best reliability.
- Naval Sheltered environment resulted in the highest failure rate.
- Elevated temperatures significantly reduced MTBF.

---

## Results Summary

### Factors Affecting Reliability

The study identified the following dominant reliability drivers:

- Temperature
- Environmental stress
- Humidity exposure
- Mechanical vibration
- Electrical stress
- Component technology

### General Conclusions

- Lower operating temperatures improve reliability.
- Ground Benign environments provide maximum component lifetime.
- Increased electrical stress accelerates degradation.
- Environmental conditions strongly influence MTBF and FIT values.
- Reliability prediction is essential for designing long-life electronic systems.
